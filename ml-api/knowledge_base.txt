- Bark with Heavy Shading • Corresponds to "BARK Inconsistently or heavily drawn". • A potential indicator of anxiety.
- Birds in Tree • May represent feelings of home, nurturing, or dependent figures in one's life. • Can also indicate a desire for freedom or escape.
- Branches Extending Upward • Generally indicates optimism, ambition, and reaching for goals. • Suggests an energetic and hopeful outlook.
- Branches Touching Ground • Can suggest feelings of being weighed down, fatigue, or depression. • May indicate a loss of ambition or energy.
- Cloud-Shaped Branches • Corresponds to 'BRANCHES "Wrapped" at ends in cloud-like balls'. • Suggests that inhibitions may be preventing the outward discharge of aggression.
- Crooked Trunk • Corresponds to a leaning or imbalanced trunk. • May suggest an imbalance of personality, fixation on the past or future, or a life path that has faced significant challenges.
- Cut or Detached Branches • Corresponds to "BRANCHES Broken, bent, or dead". • Indicates a significant psychic or physical trauma. • Can also suggest castration feelings, either psychosexual or psychosocial.
- Dead Tree • Corresponds to a "TRUNK Dead". • Indicates a feeling of crippling loss of ego control and significant psychological distress.
- Downward Drooping Branches • Can indicate feelings of sadness, discouragement, or being overwhelmed by life's pressures. • A potential sign of giving up.
- Extra Detail on Leaves • Corresponds to "LEAVES Many presented in detail". • A sign of obsessive-compulsive characteristics.
- Fantasy-Like Tree • Suggests a tendency to retreat into fantasy and imagination. • May indicate a preference for daydreaming over engaging with reality.
- Fruit on Tree • Generalizing from "APPLE TREE". • Often drawn by dependent individuals. • Can also suggest a desire for children or feelings of productivity and accomplishment.
- Hallow or Scarred Trunk • Corresponds to "SCARS". • Represents a psychic and/or physical experience that is regarded as traumatic.
- Large Tree • Corresponds to "TREE Large but contained within page". • Indicates being acutely aware of oneself in the environment. • Suggests a tendency to seek satisfaction in real-world activity rather than fantasy.
- Nest in Branches • Symbolizes the home, a need for security, and feelings of being nurtured. • Can also indicate dependency on parental figures.
- Palm Tree • Often associated with a desire for escape, relaxation, or a pleasant environment. • May be drawn by individuals feeling a need for a 'vacation' from life's pressures.
- Pine Tree • Can suggest resilience and a strong ego. • The pointy needles can sometimes be interpreted as a sign of aggression or defensiveness.
- Pointy Leaves • Often interpreted as a sign of aggression or hostility. • Can indicate a defensive or critical stance towards the environment.
- Roots • Represents the individual's grasp on reality and connection to their past. Lack of roots suggests insecurity. If roots are dead, it suggests intrapersonal imbalance and obsessive-depressive feelings. If talon-like, it suggests poor reality contact and paranoid aggression.
- Small Tree • Corresponds to "TREE Small". • Indicates feelings of inferiority and inadequacy. • Suggests a desire to withdraw from the environment.
- Thick Tree • Corresponds to "TRUNK Overly large". • Suggests feelings of environmental constriction with a tendency to react aggressively in reality or fantasy.
- Thin Tree • Corresponds to "TRUNK Very slender". • Indicates a precarious personality balance because of over-striving for satisfaction. Can also suggest feelings of inadequacy and ineptness ("TRUNK Tiny").
- Tree Split Down Middle • Corresponds to "TREE Drawn as two one-dimensional trees". • Strongly suggests a pathologic dichotomy of affect and intellect. • Organicity may be suspected.
- Tree Without Branches • Indicates a severe lack of interaction with the environment and others. • Suggests feelings of isolation, withdrawal, and a lack of resources for seeking satisfaction.
- Very Long Roots • Corresponds to "ROOTS Entering ground, overemphasis upon". • Suggests a great need to maintain a grasp of reality, often stemming from underlying feelings of insecurity.
- SIZE: Large • A drawing occupying almost all of the available space. • Indicates a feeling of frustration, great tension, and irritability. • May also be a factor in an egocentric view of one's own importance.
- SIZE: Small • An extremely small drawing. • Usually indicates a feeling of inadequacy and a tendency to withdraw from the environment.
- PLACEMENT: Left • The drawing is placed to the left of the page's midpoint. • Indicates a tendency to behave impulsively and seek immediate, emotional satisfaction. • Suggests overconcern with the past and a primary focus on the self.
- PLACEMENT: Right • The drawing is placed to the right of the page's midpoint. • Indicates stable, rigidly controlled behavior and a willingness to delay satisfaction. • Suggests a preference for intellectual over emotional satisfaction and overconcern about the future.
- PLACEMENT: Bottom • The drawing is placed below the page's midpoint. • Indicates feelings of insecurity and inadequacy, creating a depression of mood. • Suggests a tendency to be concrete and seek satisfaction in reality rather than fantasy.
- PLACEMENT: Top • The drawing is placed above the page's midpoint. • Indicates a feeling of striving for unobtainable goals. • Suggests a tendency to seek satisfaction in intellectualization or fantasy.
- PLACEMENT: Center • The drawing is centered exactly on the geometric midpoint of the page. • Indicates rigidity in behavior used to compensate for anxiety and insecurity.
- PLACEMENT: Top-Left • The drawing is placed in the upper left quadrant. • This is the "quadrant of regression," frequently used by deteriorated psychotic or organic individuals.
